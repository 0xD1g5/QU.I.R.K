# Phase 34: Motion Intelligence

## Goal

The quantum-readiness scoring engine recognizes email and broker TLS weaknesses as a distinct cryptographic surface — the `data_in_motion` subscore appears as the 6th named subscore in intelligence JSON alongside `hygiene`, `modern_tls`, `identity_trust`, `agility_signals`, and `data_at_rest`.

## Requirements Covered

- **MOTION-01** — `build_evidence_summary()` emits 6 `motion_*_count` keys (email starttls-missing, email plaintext, email weak-cipher, broker plaintext, broker weak-TLS, broker weak-cipher) plus 5 `motion_*_ratio` keys, all derived from `ep.protocol` enum strings produced by Phase 32 / 33 scanners.
- **MOTION-02** — `SCORE_WEIGHTS` gains 5 `motion_*_ratio` entries with LOCKED values: `motion_email_plaintext_ratio=12.0`, `motion_email_weak_cipher_ratio=6.0`, `motion_broker_plaintext_ratio=14.0`, `motion_broker_weak_tls_ratio=8.0`, `motion_broker_weak_cipher_ratio=6.0`.

- **MOTION-04** — `compute_readiness_score()` returns `"data_in_motion"` as the 6th `subscores` key; the overall score measurably drops when motion counters are non-zero.

1. A scan with plaintext Kafka and RabbitMQ listeners produces a strictly lower `data_in_motion` subscore than a zero-motion baseline; the `motion_broker_plaintext_ratio` (14.0) weight applies via the standard `denom` ratio path.
